02 — Research

Research Workflow

Keyword discovery → intent alignment → competitor analysis → gap identification.

Step-by-step research process

1
Seed keyword discovery
Start with 5–10 seed terms that describe your core service. Use Google Search Console (existing data), customer interviews, and sales call transcripts. Tools: Ahrefs, Semrush, or Google Keyword Planner.
2
Expand with modifiers
Layer on intent modifiers — "best," "how to," "for [industry]," "cost of," "vs," "near me." This reveals the full query landscape around your topic cluster.
3
Intent alignment — classify every keyword
Assign each keyword to an intent tier before mapping it to a page type. Mismatched intent kills rankings.
Intent Query example Best page type
Informational "what is accounts payable automation" Blog post / guide
Commercial "best AP automation software for mid-market" Service page / comparison
Transactional "AP automation software pricing" Service page + CTA
Navigational "[brand] login" Homepage / app
4
Competitor SERP analysis
For each target keyword, manually review the top 5 results. Note: content length, H2 structure, SERP features (featured snippets, PAA), and domain authority of ranking pages. This sets your quality bar.
5
Content gap identification
Use Ahrefs Content Gap or Semrush Keyword Gap to find terms competitors rank for that you don't. Also check "People Also Ask" boxes and "Related searches" at the bottom of Google results for hidden intent clusters.
6
Keyword prioritisation matrix
Score each keyword on three axes, then rank your list.
Factor High priority Low priority
Business value Directly maps to revenue Tangentially related
Search volume 500+ monthly searches <50 monthly searches
Keyword difficulty Low-medium (<50 KD) Very high (>70 KD)
⚠️
Common Pitfall
Targeting only high-volume vanity keywords. A 200-search/month keyword with low difficulty and high commercial intent will deliver more pipeline than a 10,000-search keyword dominated by enterprise SaaS brands with DR 85+.
03 — Technical

On-Page Optimisation Playbook

Every element, every rule — ready to execute.

Element Rule / Specification Example
Title Tag 50–60 chars. Primary keyword near the front. Brand at the end with a pipe. AP Automation Software for Mid-Market | Acme
Meta Description 140–160 chars. Summarise value prop + include a soft CTA. Not a ranking factor but drives CTR. Automate invoice processing in 30 days. Trusted by 200+ finance teams. See how it works →

04 — Framework

Content Frameworks

Proven structure for service pages and blog posts — fill in the blanks.

Service page framework

The 7-section service page
H1
Hero — Audience + Pain + Promise
Headline names the audience, acknowledges the problem, and states the outcome. Sub-headline adds proof ("used by 500+ B2B companies"). Primary CTA above the fold.
1
Problem framing
2–3 short paragraphs naming specific pain points your buyer experiences. Use language from sales calls and reviews — not marketing copy.
2
Solution overview
Explain your service in plain English. How does it work? What's the process or delivery model? Include a simple 3–5 step process list.
3
Benefits (not features)
3–6 benefit-led statements. Format: [Action verb] + [Outcome] + [Time frame]. Example: "Reduce invoice processing time by 70% in your first month."
4
Proof elements
Case study snippet, client logos, a testimonial with name and role, or a specific stat. At least one proof element per page — no proof = no trust.
5
FAQ section (schema target)
5–8 questions pulled from PAA boxes and sales call objections. Answers: 2–4 sentences each.
6
CTA close
Repeat the primary CTA with a fresh angle — risk reversal, free trial, or a specific next step. "Book a 20-minute demo — no pressure, no sales deck."

Blog post framework

The AIDA-SEO blog structure
A
Intro — hook + promise (100–150 words)
Open with the problem or a surprising stat. State exactly what the reader will learn. No "In this article, we will discuss..." fluff.
I
Background / context (optional)
Brief definition or history only if the audience needs it. Advanced readers? Skip it. Intermediate? One paragraph max.
D
Core body — H2 sections answering sub-questions
Each H2 targets a secondary keyword or a PAA question. Each section: 200–400 words with a specific takeaway. Use numbered lists, bullet points, or tables where scannable beats prose.
A
Action — CTA embedded mid-page + conclusion
Place a soft CTA naturally within the body. Conclude with a summary and a link to a related service page.
05 — Craft

Writing Best Practices

Tone, readability, E-E-A-T, CTAs, and what to avoid.

Tone & Voice

B2B ≠ boring. Write like a knowledgeable colleague, not a brochure. Active voice. Short sentences (<20 words average). Direct address ("you").

Good: "You can cut processing time in half."
Bad: "Processing times may potentially be reduced for customers."
Readability Targets

Service pages: Flesch-Kincaid Grade 9–11. Blog posts: Grade 7–9. Test with Hemingway App.

Short paragraphs (3–4 lines) Subheadings every 300 words Bullets for 3+ items

E-E-A-T signals — what to include

Signal How to demonstrate it
Experience Case studies, specific project examples, real screenshots, "we've seen" language
Expertise Author bios with credentials, accurate industry terminology, original analysis or data
Authoritativeness Third-party citations, backlinks from industry publications, media mentions, awards
Trustworthiness Accurate information with sources, transparent pricing or process, real client testimonials with full names, clear contact details

CTA strategy

Service pages — primary CTA

One dominant action per page. Make it specific and low-commitment: "Book a 20-min demo" beats "Contact us."

Above the foldAfter proof sectionPage footer
Blog — soft CTAs

Inline text links, content upgrades (downloadable checklist), or a related article recommendation at the bottom.

Mid-content (contextual)End of article
⚠️
Keyword Stuffing — Avoid This
Using the exact-match keyword more than once per 100–150 words is a signal of over-optimisation. Use synonyms, related terms, and natural language. Google's NLP understands semantic relevance — you don't need to repeat "AP automation software" 15 times.

Keyword density guidance

Keyword type Frequency target
Primary keyword Title, H1, first 100 words, 1× per 300 words in body, URL
Secondary keywords 1–2 H2s, naturally throughout body copy
LSI / semantic terms Weave throughout — no forced count
06 — Process

Team & Process Workflow

From brief to published — and what goes wrong along the way.

Content production workflow

1
Content brief (SEO lead, 1–2 hrs)
Document target keyword, secondary keywords, search intent, target persona, word count, competitor URLs to beat, H2 outline, internal links to include, and the desired CTA. Templatise this brief — it saves 40% of revision cycles.
2
Writer draft (copywriter, 2–4 hrs)
Writer follows the brief strictly. First draft submitted with a self-audit note: keyword placement, CTA location, word count, and any gaps flagged by the writer.
3
SEO review (SEO lead, 45 min)
Check: keyword placement, meta fields, internal link targets, header structure, E-E-A-T elements, CTA copy and placement. Use the on-page checklist in Section 8.
4
Subject matter expert review (SME, 30 min)
Verify accuracy, check for outdated data, and confirm claims are defensible. This is the E and A in E-E-A-T. Non-negotiable for YMYL-adjacent B2B content.
5
Final edit + publish
Copy-edit pass, image alt text, schema markup, canonical tag, and internal link implementation. Publish → submit URL to Google Search Console for indexing.
6
30-day ranking check + optimisation
After 30 days, check position. Pages ranking 5–15 get an optimisation pass: refresh intro, add internal links, update stats, expand FAQs section.

Common pitfalls & mitigations

Pitfall Symptom Mitigation
No brief Writer misses keywords, wrong intent, too long/short Mandatory brief template before any writing starts
Publishing and forgetting Pages drop after initial boost Quarterly content audit + refresh cycle
Keyword cannibalisation Two pages fight for same keyword Keyword-to-URL mapping spreadsheet maintained by SEO lead
Ignoring search intent High impressions, low CTR, high bounce SERP analysis before any page is briefed
Over-optimisation Keyword stuffing, unnatural H2s Read the page aloud — if it sounds robotic, it reads robotic

Sample monthly report structure

1
Executive summary (1 paragraph)
Month-over-month change in organic sessions, conversions, and top keyword movements. Written for a non-technical stakeholder.
2
Traffic & engagement metrics
Organic sessions vs prior month and prior year. Engagement rate, dwell time, bounce rate. Top 10 pages by organic traffic.
3
Keyword ranking movements
New keywords entering top 10. Keywords improving from 11–30 into top 10. Keywords declining (flagged for refresh).
4
Conversion performance
Organic conversion rate vs paid and direct. Top converting pages. Any A/B test results on CTA copy or placement.
5
Actions for next month
3–5 specific actions: pages to refresh, new content to publish, technical fixes to implement, internal linking opportunities.
